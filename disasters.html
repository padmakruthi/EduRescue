Disaster Information & Guides

Learn how to prepare for and respond to various types of disasters with our comprehensive guides. Knowledge is the first step to safety.

Earthquake

During an earthquake, remember the "Drop, Cover, Hold On" protocol. Find a sturdy piece of furniture to take cover under and stay away from windows, mirrors, or anything that could fall on you. If you are outdoors, move to an open area away from buildings and power lines.

Flood

Never walk or drive through floodwaters. Even shallow water can hide dangers like strong currents or damaged roads. If you are advised to evacuate, do so immediately and move to higher ground. Turn off all utilities at the main switch to prevent electrical hazards.

Fire

In case of a fire, always activate the fire alarm and evacuate the building immediately. Never use an elevator during a fire. If there is smoke, stay low to the ground and crawl to an exit, as cleaner air is near the floor. Once outside, do not re-enter the building.

Cyclone / Hurricane

Secure your home by closing and securing all windows and doors. Stay indoors and away from windows. If you live in a low-lying or coastal area, evacuate early and move to a designated cyclone shelter or a safe location inland.

Tsunami

If you are in a coastal area and feel a strong earthquake or hear a tsunami warning, move to higher ground as quickly as possible. Do not wait for an official alert. A sudden recession of the sea is a natural warning sign; head inland immediately.

Landslide

During a landslide, move out of the path of the slide as quickly as you can. Avoid river valleys and low-lying areas, and take shelter behind a sturdy object or a stand of trees. If you are in a car, do not try to outrun the slide; get out and seek higher ground.
